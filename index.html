Meet Our Giraffes

Frankie

West African Giraffe

  • Born: February 27, 2011
  • Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis peralta.
  • One of the rarest giraffe subspecies, with a population that once dropped to as few as 49 individuals in the 1990s.Thanks to conservation efforts, their numbers have increased, and today there are over 600 in the wild.
More About Giraffes!

Coconut

Rothschild Giraffe

  • Born: February 27, 2021
  • They live in semi-arid regions, such as savannas, and are typically found near water sources in these areas.
  • Regions: Niger and parts of Chad, Mali, and Burkina Faso.
  • Like other giraffes, they form loose herds that can change frequently.

Meet Our Monkeys

Cookie

Red-Shanked Douc Langur Monkey

  • Born: December 29, 2018
  • Scientific Name: Pygathrix nemaeus
  • Region: Vietnam, Cambodia, and parts of Laos.
  • The Red-Shanked Douc Langurs nickname is "Queen of the Primates" due to their vibrant and diverse color palette.
  • Long tail (about 60 cm / 24 in), which helps with balance but is not prehensile.
More About Red-Shanked Douc Monkeys!

Earl

Red-Shanked Douc Langur Monkey

  • Born: July 14, 2018
  • Lives in evergreen, semi-evergreen, and limestone forests, usually at elevations up to 1,600 meters (5,250 feet).
  • Lives in groups of 4 to 50 individuals, consisting of one dominant male and multiple females with offspring.
  • Uses vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language for communication.
More About Red-Shanked Douc monkeys!

Banana Pudding

Red-Shanked Douc Langur Monkeys

  • Born: December 29, 2024
  • After a 165-190 day gestation period, females give birth to one infant.
  • Critically Endangered (IUCN Red List) due to deforestation & habitat loss, hunting & poaching for the illegal pet trade and traditional medicine and Vietnam War deforestation and bombing affected their populations.

Meet Our Alligators

Wren

American Alligator

  • Hatched: May 2, 2015
  • Wren is 10 feet long
  • Early alligator ancestors appeared more than 200 million years ago.
  • Large, dark greenish-black reptiles with a broad, rounded snout.
  • Unlike crocodiles, their upper teeth are not visible when the mouth is closed.
More About Alligators!

Aspen

American Alligator

  • Hatched: September 15, 2024
  • Scientific Name: Alligator mississippiensis
  • Region: Southeastern United States
  • Aspen is a rare albino alligator. There are only 12 known alibino alligators in the world.
